        I think “condescension” is a little strong. I think Ms. Block understands that a large core of Swifts audience is composed of teenage girls and she wanted to know whether Swift had any interest in broadening her themes beyond typical, teenage love angst, bubble gum pop. There’s nothing wrong with songs like “We Are Never Ever Getting Back Together” and “Trouble”, and there’s nothing condescending about characterizing someone’s music accurately. The Beatles hit big early with songs like “I Wanna Hold Your Hand”; nothing condescending about characterizing that as bubble gum pop, and Lennon/McCartney grew to be some of the most accomplished songwriters in the history of music. Swift was very reasoned, and reasonable in her answer; indicated that she’s interested in broadening her themes and gave very thoughtful insight into some of the ideas that she finds important. The whole thing was a fair exchange.

        If I’d criticize anything, it’s that Block missed an opportunity to get some insight into an incredibly successful young businesswoman’s experiences in the industry. That’s the type of inside dope any young person could use. And that Taylor Swift has to be at least as inspiring to young people as the musician.
