THE CANDELIGHT PLANTER

This planter was created to assist homeless people living underground sustain themselves. It uses candlelight reflected using mirrors, which then trancends through daylight fitlers onto the plants. It has a refrigerater in the bottom pocket that keeps plant fresh using salt or wet sand.

FIRST DRAFT

My first draft uses a bent plexi glass mirror,a clear plexi candle shelf, a wool pot, a wood shell and a wet sand refrigerator. The wool pot soaks up the water to allwo the plant to continue to absorb the water so one can water it less frequently. Each piece uses the same 60 degree angles so

SECOND DRAFT

This draft I hung the pot in order to conserve space, there are different holes on the side which allow you to raise and lower the pot depending on the size of the plant. However this version does not include a refrigerator, it also cuts down the cost of the shell.

THIRD DRAFT

This draft was my second attempt at cutting down costs. Instead of using a plexi glass mirror it uses scrap mirros that are then smashed and put into a mosiac.

FOURTH DRAFT

My final draft changed the wooden base to cardbaord in order to allow for a refrigerator and cut down costs. Intead of wet sand which would disinigrate the cardboard the base is filled with salt, which also acts as a water repellent for the wetness coming from watering the plants. Instead of a wool pot there are reused containers that are found objects that float in the sand.
